Ryan received his Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Maryland in 2016. There, he searched for evidence of the most extreme particle acceleration in the universe in the largest explosions known (gamma-ray bursts). To do this, he used the largest particle detector on Earth---called IceCube and located at the South Pole---to find evidence of the production of hard-to-find particles called neutrinos, which would be produced during the particle acceleration. His analysis found no evidence in a never-before-searched high energy channel, validating previous results and yielding the world's best limits to neutrino production in gamma-ray bursts. Outside of physics and data science, Ryan loves to travel the world with his wife Megan, as well as see live concerts of their favorite bands.
